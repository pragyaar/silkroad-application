02 — Platform Audit

Three things
I noticed
in your feed.

01
Brand Consistency
The Open Mic Night post lives on a different planet.

The April 23 Open Mic Night post — the one with the cassette tapes and hot-pink retro palette — looks like it was made by a different organization. Against the dark olive, cream, and Sora-heavy grid, it's a jarring visual break. Your grid is your brand. Every post is a tile in it.

Every post — even community events that feel casual — should run through the same brand template. That doesn't mean stiff. It means the Open Mic Night post can be warm, retro, and alive AND still feel like Silk Road made it.

02
Content Lifecycle
Your events die the day they happen.

The Literary Festival ran September 20–21. The Open Mic Night was April 23. Looking at the feed: no recap posts, no quote cards from speakers, no "the room was full" moments captured afterward. Events get pre-promotion then disappear.

Post-event content is often more powerful than pre-event. One speaker quote card, one crowd photo with a caption, one "here's what we heard" carousel — each of these extends the event's life by weeks and gives donors something to point to.

03
Speaker Series
Haroon Siddiqui, Waubgeshig Rice, Uzma Jalaluddin — but no series.

You've featured three exceptional Literary Festival speakers as individual one-off graphic posts. They have no connective tissue — no consistent series name, no recurring format, no hook that tells followers "this is a thing we do."

Turn speaker features into a named series — something like "Voices at the Festival" — with a consistent template. That way each new speaker reveal builds anticipation for the next one, and followers know what to expect. You're already doing the work; it just needs a frame.

05 — Two Content Ideas

Specific.
Not generic.

These two ideas come directly from patterns I observed in your existing content — not from a list of "Instagram tips for nonprofits."

01
Instagram · Named Series
"Voices at the Festival" — Speaker Series

You already feature Literary Festival speakers as individual posts. The gap is that each post looks different and has no series identity. This idea takes what you're doing and wraps a consistent template and name around it — so every reveal builds on the last one, and followers start to anticipate the next drop. One Canva template. Repeatable every week through September.

Observed: Speaker posts for Haroon Siddiqui, Waubgeshig Rice, Uzma Jalaluddin — same idea, no connective tissue.
02
Instagram Stories + Email
"What [Speaker] Wants You to Read" — Pre-Festival Reading Lists

Four to six weeks before the festival, ask each confirmed speaker for 3 books they'd recommend before their talk. Post as a carousel on Instagram, link to them in the newsletter, pin it to a Festival Stories Highlight. It's free content that the speakers will want to share, it drives organic reach, and it gives your audience something to do while they wait for September.

Observed: No pre-festival warming content visible in the feed. The gap between speaker announcement and ticket urgency is long and silent.
